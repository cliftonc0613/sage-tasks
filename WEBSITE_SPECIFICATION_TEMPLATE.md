## üéØ StoryBrand Framework

### 1. The Customer (Hero)
**Who is your ideal customer?**
- Demographics:
- Pain points:
- Goals and aspirations:
- Where do they spend time online?

### 2. The Problem
**What problems does your customer face?**
- **External Problem:** (Physical/tangible problem they need solved)
- **Internal Problem:** (Frustration/doubt they feel about the external problem)  
- **Philosophical Problem:** (Why this problem matters on a deeper level)

### 3. The Guide (Your Business)
**How do you position yourself as the trusted guide?**
- **Empathy:** "We understand your frustration with..."
- **Authority:** Credentials, certifications, years of experience, success stories

### 4. The Plan
**What 3-step plan do you give customers?**
1. **Step 1:** [Simple first action - usually "Contact us" or "Get quote"]
2. **Step 2:** [What happens next - consultation, assessment, etc.]
3. **Step 3:** [The outcome - completed work, satisfaction guaranteed]

### 5. Call to Action
- **Direct CTA:** Primary action (Call now, Get quote, Schedule service)
- **Transitional CTA:** Lower commitment option (Download guide, Get tips)

### 6. Success
**What does life look like after they use your service?**
- Specific outcomes and benefits
- Emotional satisfaction
- Time/money saved

### 7. Failure
**What negative consequences happen if they don't act?**
- Problems get worse
- Costs increase
- Missed opportunities

## üé® Design Direction

### Brand Personality
**Authentic Contractor Feel - Professional, Not Corporate**

- **Trustworthy:** Real business owner, local presence, proven track record
- **Competent:** Quality work, attention to detail, professional standards
- **Approachable:** Friendly communication, easy to work with, responsive
- **Reliable:** Shows up on time, keeps promises, follows through

### Visual Identity
- **Color Palette:** 
  - Primary: [Professional blues, greens, or industry-appropriate colors]
  - Secondary: [Complementary accent colors]
  - Neutral: [Grays and whites for balance]
  - Avoid: Overly bright or "corporate" colors

- **Typography:**
  - Headlines: Strong, readable fonts (Open Sans, Roboto, Source Sans Pro)
  - Body text: Clean, professional fonts
  - Avoid: Decorative or overly stylized fonts

- **Photography Style:**
  - Real work photos, not stock images
  - Before/after shots
  - Team/owner photos
  - Equipment and process shots
  - High quality, well-lit, professional

### Layout Principles
- Clean, uncluttered design
- Mobile-first responsive
- Clear hierarchy and navigation
- Prominent contact information
- Easy-to-find service information

## üìù Content Structure

### Homepage
1. **Hero Section**
   - Clear headline with value proposition
   - Subheading explaining what you do
   - Primary call-to-action button
   - Hero image (real work photo)
   - Trust indicators (years in business, certifications)

2. **Problem/Solution Section**
   - Address customer pain points
   - Position your service as the solution
   - 2-3 key benefits

3. **Services Overview**
   - 3-6 main service categories
   - Brief description for each
   - Link to detailed service pages

4. **Social Proof Section**
   - Customer testimonials
   - Before/after photos
   - Google reviews integration
   - Industry certifications

5. **About Section Preview**
   - Owner photo and brief story
   - Why they started the business
   - What makes them different
   - Link to full About page

6. **Local Area Coverage**
   - Service areas/locations
   - Local landmarks or references
   - Community involvement

7. **Contact Section**
   - Phone number prominently displayed
   - Contact form
   - Business hours
   - Emergency availability (if applicable)

### Service Pages
For each major service:
- **Service-specific headline**
- **Detailed service description**
- **Process overview (3-step plan)**
- **Pricing information (when appropriate)**
- **Before/after gallery**
- **Service-specific testimonials**
- **Related services**
- **Strong call-to-action**

### About Page
- **Owner's story and background**
- **Why they started the business**
- **Team members (if applicable)**
- **Certifications and qualifications**
- **Community involvement**
- **Mission and values**
- **Personal touch (family, hobbies, local connections)**

### Contact Page
- **Multiple contact methods**
- **Contact form**
- **Service area map**
- **Business hours**
- **Emergency contact (if applicable)**
- **Office/warehouse location**
- **Response time expectations**
